The Speakers

In-depth technical sessions from PowerShell experts

Jeffrey Snover

Distinguished Engineer and Inventor of Windows PowerShell, Microsoft

Jeffrey Snover is a Distinguished Engineer Microsoft and the Lead Architect for the Windows Server and System Center Division. Snover is the inventor of Windows PowerShell, an object-based distributed automation engine, scripting language, and command line shell. Snover joined Microsoft in 1999 as divisional architect for the Management and Services Division, providing technical direction across Microsoft's management technologies and products. Snover has over 30 years of industry experience with a focus on management technologies and solutions. He was an architect in the office of the CTO at Tivoli and a development manager at NetView. He has worked also as a consulting engineer and development manager at DEC, where he led various network and systems management projects. Snover held 8 patents prior to joining Microsoft, and has registered over 30 patents since. He is a frequent speaker at industry and research conferences on a variety of management and language topics.

Narayanan (Nana) Lakshmanan

Lead Engineer, Windows PowerShell, Microsoft

Narayanan (Nana) Lakshmanan is a Principal Software Engineering Manager for Windows PowerShell Desired State Configuration (DSC). Nana has over 15 years of experience in the industry. He is with the PowerShell team since its early days. Nana led the development team for DSC in PowerShell 4.0 and also led Group Policy. Prior to that he was a developer in the PowerShell team. In PowerShell 3.0, he worked on PowerShell workflows and was responsible for its connection/activity management and cross layer performance/scalability. Earlier, in PowerShell 2.0, Nana worked on PowerShell remoting and owned its protocol, experience and the PowerShell job model. In PowerShell 1.0, he worked on the formatting subsystem and packaging. Before joining Microsoft he worked in India for Hewlett Packard and others. Nana holds a Masters in Software Systems from BITS, Pilani and a Bachelors in Electronics and Communications Engineering from University of Madras.

Windows Desired State Configuration

Benjamin Hodge
Kemp Technologies

Are you paying attention to DevOps? Adoption of DevOps practices can greatly improve your company’s deployment efficiency. PowerShell Desired State Configuration (DSC) helps teams take the management of their Windows-based infrastructure into the DevOps space by capturing their infrastructure as "code". The declarative PowerShell model enables autonomous, and transparent configuration and deployment of Windows infrastructure and components. Capturing infrastructure as "code" is not only a means to manage what they have, at scale and speed, it is also a way to decouple the complexity of their existing environment in order to facilitate a migration to the cloud. Come see how DSC works and how you can use it to make configuration of internal applications, databases, and other infrastructure elements more efficient.

Manage your server securely using PowerShell Web Access and custom constrained endpoints

Ritesh Modi
Microsoft

In the current world of Information Technology, protective measures do not stop at the network edge. There have been reports to indicate the need to protect assets using measures that reduce administrative access. While the principle of least privilege has always been known to IT Security professionals, there is a need in the industry for a standardized method of constructing an operator experience that reduces access with a more sophisticated level of granularity than what is available in many traditional access control models. Custom Remoting Constrained endpoints helps us to achieve that. PowerShell Web Access (PSWA) feature in Windows Server 2012 allows remote administrative access to a set of servers with granular delegated access and only expose specific administrative privileges to different levels to support teams. PowerShell Web Access combined with custom constrained endpoints bring you a great way to manage and delegate admin access remotely. This level 400 demo heavy session will delve into the details about using Powershell Web Access along with Remoting constrained endpoints.

This session will feature some great demos to demonstrate the power of Providers using DELL PowerShell Provider and how easy it is going to get managing Dell systems (primarily client systems). As a natural next step to management using PowerShell, I will briefly demonstrate how DSC can be wrapped around the existing implementation.
